Expert General Medicine Care at Sangameshwar Hospital, Gadag

The General Medicine Department at Sangameshwar Hospital serves as the first point of contact for patients seeking medical care in Gadag. Our experienced general physicians specialize in the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of a wide range of adult health conditions, from common infections and lifestyle diseases to chronic illnesses like diabetes, hypertension, and thyroid disorders. We provide evidence-based, personalized treatment plans, ensuring early detection and effective management of health concerns. Whether you’re experiencing fever, fatigue, body pain, or an undiagnosed condition, our team ensures timely and trusted care.

Conditions We Treat

Our General Medicine team is equipped to manage a broad spectrum of medical conditions, including but not limited to:

  • Fever, viral infections, and seasonal flu
  • Diabetes and blood sugar-related disorders
  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • Respiratory infections and asthma
  • Gastrointestinal issues such as acidity, ulcers, and constipation
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Headaches, migraines, and general fatigue
  • Joint pain and mild musculoskeletal issues
  • Anemia and vitamin deficiencies
  • Lifestyle-related health concerns

Common Procedures & Services

Our general physicians use a comprehensive approach to care and are trained to perform and recommend a wide range of procedures and diagnostic tests:

  • Physical examinations and routine health check-ups
  • Chronic disease management and counseling
  • Blood pressure and blood sugar monitoring
  • ECG (Electrocardiogram)
  • Basic wound care and infection management
  • Nebulization therapy for respiratory issues
  • Vaccination and immunization (adult)
  • Preventive health screenings

When to Visit a General Physician

You should consult our General Medicine Department if you experience:

  • Persistent or recurring fever
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness
  • Chronic body pain or headaches
  • Symptoms of respiratory or digestive issues
  • Uncontrolled blood sugar or blood pressure
  • Signs of thyroid dysfunction (e.g., weight changes, fatigue)
  • Need for routine health check-ups or vaccinations
  • Undiagnosed symptoms requiring evaluation
