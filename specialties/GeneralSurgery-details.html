General Surgeon in Gadag – Safe and Skilled Surgical Care at Sangameshwar Hospital

The General Surgery Department at Sangameshwar Hospital provides safe, effective, and compassionate surgical care for a wide range of health conditions. Our team of skilled general surgeons performs both emergency and planned (elective) surgeries using modern techniques to ensure faster recovery and minimal discomfort. From treating hernias and gallstones to managing appendicitis and soft tissue swellings, we focus on precise diagnosis, careful surgical planning, and thorough post-operative care. Our goal is to help patients in Gadag and surrounding areas access high-quality surgical services without traveling to distant cities.

Conditions We Treat

Our surgical team is skilled in diagnosing and treating a variety of conditions requiring operative care, including but not limited to:

  • Hernias (inguinal, umbilical, incisional)
  • Gallstones and gallbladder disease
  • Appendicitis
  • Piles (haemorrhoids)
  • Fissures and fistulas
  • Breast lumps
  • Lipomas and cysts
  • Thyroid swellings
  • Varicose veins
  • Minor trauma and injuries requiring surgery

Common Procedures & Services

Our general surgeons offer a variety of surgical procedures, using both open and minimally invasive techniques:

  • Hernia repair (open and laparoscopic)
  • Gallbladder removal (laparoscopic cholecystectomy)
  • Appendectomy
  • Haemorrhoidectomy (piles surgery)
  • Fissure surgery
  • Excision of lumps, cysts, and tumours
  • Abscess drainage
  • Biopsies
  • Emergency trauma surgery
  • Wound repair and care

When to Visit a General Surgeon

You should consult our General Surgery Department if you experience:

  • Persistent abdominal pain or swelling
  • Lumps or growths in the abdomen, breast, or elsewhere
  • Symptoms of appendicitis (e.g., severe abdominal pain, fever)
  • Hernia symptoms (e.g., bulge, discomfort when lifting)
  • Pain or bleeding from the anal region (piles or fissures)
  • Gallstone symptoms (e.g., pain after meals, nausea)
  • Injuries requiring surgical intervention
  • Varicose veins causing pain or swelling
