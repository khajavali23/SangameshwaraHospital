Kidney Stones: When to Visit a Hospital and How Laser Treatment Helps

Kidney stones are one of the most common urological problems seen today. They can affect anyone, from young adults to older people, and the pain can be extremely severe. While some stones are small and may pass naturally, others require medical attention and advanced treatment. At Dr Dhannur’s Sangameshwar Hospital, Gadag, patients have access to modern facilities such as laser treatment for kidney stones, making care safer, faster, and more effective.

What Are Kidney Stones?

Kidney stones are hard deposits that form inside the kidneys when minerals and salts in the urine come together. They can vary in size- some may be as small as a grain of sand, while others can grow large enough to block the urinary tract.

The most common causes include:

  • Not drinking enough water
  • Diet high in salt or processed foods
  • Family history of stones
  • Certain medical conditions

Common Symptoms You Should Not Ignore

Kidney stones often make their presence known through very clear signs. Some of the most common symptoms are:

  • Sudden, sharp pain in the back or side
  • Pain while passing urine
  • Blood in the urine (reddish or brownish urine)
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Urgent or frequent need to urinate

If you are facing any of these symptoms, it is important not to delay visiting a doctor.

When to Visit a Hospital for Kidney Stones

While mild cases can sometimes be managed at home, there are situations where immediate hospital care is necessary:

  • Severe or unbearable pain
  • Fever or chills (could indicate infection)
  • Difficulty or inability to pass urine
  • Repeated kidney stone episodes

At Sangameshwar Hospital, Gadag, our 24x7 emergency services, urology department, and advanced diagnostic facilities ensure that patients get timely and accurate treatment.

Treatment Options for Kidney Stones

Treatment depends on the size and location of the stone:

  • Small Stones: Often pass naturally with increased water intake and prescribed medication.
  • Medium or Large Stones: May require procedures such as laser surgery for safe removal.

How Laser Treatment Works

Laser treatment is one of the most advanced and effective methods for removing kidney stones. Here’s how it works:

  • A thin, flexible scope is passed into the urinary tract.
  • A laser is used to break the stone into very small pieces.
  • These fragments are either removed or pass naturally through urine.

Benefits of laser treatment at Sangameshwar Hospital include:

  • Minimally invasive (no large cuts)
  • Less pain compared to traditional surgery
  • Faster recovery time
  • Shorter hospital stay
  • Reduced risk of complications

Preventing Kidney Stones in the Future

Once you’ve had kidney stones, there is a chance they can come back. However, simple lifestyle changes can reduce the risk:

  • Drink 8–10 glasses of water every day
  • Limit salty and processed foods
  • Include fresh fruits and vegetables in your diet
  • Avoid excess intake of sugar-sweetened drinks
  • Go for regular check-ups if you have a history of stones

Conclusion

Kidney stones can cause extreme discomfort, but with modern medical facilities, they can be treated safely and effectively.
